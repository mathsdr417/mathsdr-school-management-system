# Discipline and Behaviour Management Policy

## Overview
The school is committed to maintaining a safe, orderly, and respectful learning environment that promotes positive behaviour, self-discipline, and responsibility among learners.

Discipline is applied in a fair, consistent, and child-centred manner.

## Scope
This policy applies to:
- All learners enrolled in the school
- Staff members responsible for learner supervision
- School activities conducted on and off campus

## Behaviour Expectations
Learners are expected to:
- Show respect to staff, peers, and visitors
- Obey school rules and regulations
- Attend school regularly and punctually
- Care for school property and learning materials
- Conduct themselves responsibly at all times

## Prohibited Behaviour
Unacceptable behaviour includes, but is not limited to:
- Bullying, harassment, or intimidation
- Fighting or violent conduct
- Disrespectful language or behaviour
- Theft or damage to property
- Truancy or persistent lateness

## Disciplinary Measures
Disciplinary actions may include:
- Verbal warnings and counselling
- Written warnings
- Detention or corrective tasks
- Parent or guardian engagement
- Behaviour support plans
- Suspension (where necessary and in line with regulations)

Corporal punishment is not permitted.

## Positive Behaviour Support
The school promotes positive behaviour through:
- Clear communication of expectations
- Recognition and reinforcement of good behaviour
- Guidance and counselling support
- Collaborative problem-solving with learners and parents

## Role of Parents and Guardians
Parents and guardians are encouraged to:
- Support school discipline policies
- Cooperate with school authorities in addressing behaviour concerns
- Reinforce positive behaviour at home

## Policy Review
This policy is reviewed periodically to ensure effectiveness, fairness, and alignment with child-protection standards.
